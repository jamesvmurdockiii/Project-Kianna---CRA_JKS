## Strongest Claim

CRA has a bounded v2.3 host-side software baseline for a generic bounded recurrent continuous-state interface: on the locked 8000-step Mackey-Glass/Lorenz/NARMA10 public scoreboard it improves aggregate geomean MSE versus v2.2, beats lag-only and random-reservoir online controls, narrows the ESN gap, preserves the Tier 7.0i topology nonclaim, and passes full NEST compact regression.

## Claim Boundaries

- v2.3 is host-side software evidence for a generic bounded recurrent continuous-state interface.
- v2.3 does not prove topology-specific recurrence; Tier 7.0i explicitly failed/narrowed that claim.
- v2.3 does not beat ESN on the locked public benchmark scoreboard; ESN remains the strongest aggregate baseline.
- v2.3 does not prove hardware transfer or native/on-chip recurrence.
- v2.3 does not prove language, long-horizon planning, AGI, or ASI.
